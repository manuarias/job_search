# Step 2: Recruiter Eye Test — Vantegrate Salesforce Developer Junior (VANT)

**Role:** Senior Technical Recruiter (10+ years, 200+ resumes/week)
**Hiring Manager's Top 3 Must-Haves:**
1. **Salesforce development** — Apex, Flows, LWC, SOQL
2. **n8n / workflow automation** — webhooks, HTTP nodes, error handling
3. **APIs + Autonomous execution** — REST, OAuth, OOP, ability to take a requirement and run with it

---

## 👀 7-Second Glance

**First impression:** *"This guy is a Project Leader at Mercado Libre with 10+ years. Why is he applying for a Junior Salesforce role?"*

**What stands out:**
- **Mercado Libre** — instantly recognizable brand in LatAm tech
- **Project Leader** title — signals seniority, ownership, scale
- **10+ years** experience — way above the "Junior" bracket
- **n8n** — bolded in the summary, catches attention because it's rare and the JD specifically wants it

**Must-have signals detected:**
- ✅ n8n — visible immediately
- ✅ APIs / REST — in the skills section
- ✅ Agile / autonomy — implied by leadership role
- ❌ **Salesforce** — completely invisible. Not a single Salesforce keyword.

**Initial verdict:** *"Interesting misfit. The n8n + API profile is a strong match, but there's zero Salesforce on this resume. Either this guy is pivoting hard, or he didn't read the JD. Needs a deeper look."*

---

## 🔍 30-Second Deeper Scan

### Strongest Signal (The Hook)

**n8n automation at Mercado Libre scale.** The candidate didn't just "use" n8n — he "implemented n8n and AI automations" to remove administrative bottlenecks, increasing coding bandwidth and delivery velocity for a squad of 9 handling **20,000+ RPM**. This is exactly the kind of impact the JD is looking for under "Automatización de procesos" and "n8n y automatización". Most applicants will have "I used n8n for small workflows." This candidate *architected automation at scale for one of the largest e-commerce platforms in Latin America.*

### Weakest Signal / Red Flag

**Zero Salesforce.** The entire CV reads like a backend/platform engineering profile — Java, microservices, APIs, CI/CD, team leadership. There is no mention of Apex, Flows, LWC, SOQL, or any Salesforce platform experience. For a role called "Salesforce Developer," this is a gaping hole. The JD says "contamos con programas de capacitación interna," which is an opening — but the recruiter has to notice and believe that.

### Second concern: **Overqualification.** The JD says "Junior" and "cursando a partir del segundo año universitario o al menos un año de experiencia." This candidate has 10+ years and was a Project Leader. The recruiter might think: *"He'll be bored in 3 months. He's using this as a stepping stone. He'll demand a senior salary."*

### One Change to Increase Shortlist Probability

**Add a "Salesforce & Platform Engineering" framing to the summary.** If the first thing the recruiter reads is something like: *"Engineering leader pivoting to Salesforce development — combining 10+ years of backend/API experience at Mercado Libre with hands-on n8n automation and a deep commitment to learning the Salesforce ecosystem (Apex, Flows, LWC)."* — that immediately addresses both the Salesforce gap AND frames the seniority as an asset, not a risk.

## 🧠 Key Narrative Insight

**This role is a platform pivot, not a step down.** The candidate needs to frame it as: *"I've mastered backend engineering and automation at scale. Now I want to apply that depth to the Salesforce ecosystem, where my n8n + API + OOP foundation lets me hit the ground running while I ramp up on Apex and Flows."* That turns the #1 weakness (zero Salesforce) into a story about deliberate career expansion — much more compelling than a generic "I want to learn Salesforce" pitch.
