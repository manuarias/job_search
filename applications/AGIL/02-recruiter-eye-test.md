## Key Insight About Narrative Framing

**The Story Is There, But It's Buried Under Safe Language**

This CV reads like someone who's trying not to overstate their role. Here's what's happening:

### What's Actually True (But Not Said)
- This person **WAS** a Technical Program Manager at MELI — they led a cross-functional squad, owned the "Kits" product initiative, managed dependencies with Core Architecture, and improved delivery processes. That's TPM work.
- They **HAVE** systems thinking — the "Shadow Processing & Debugging Engine" is a sophisticated solution to a complex problem.
- They **ARE** data-driven — "70% reduction", "20,000+ RPM", team of 9.

### What's Said Instead
- "Project Leader" (sounds junior to TPM)
- "Spearheaded" (good) but "Orchestrated high-stakes negotiations" (fluffy)
- "Championed the development" (weak — did you lead it or cheer for it?)

### The Fix
**Own the TPM title.** At Mercado Libre, a "Project Leader" managing a squad of 9 developers + 2 Tech Leads, owning product delivery, and interfacing with architecture teams IS a Technical Program Manager. Call it what it is.

**Reframe the narrative:**
- ❌ "Project Leader" → ✅ "Technical Program Manager" (or at minimum "Engineering Manager")
- ❌ "Spearheaded the end-to-end delivery" → ✅ "Owned end-to-end delivery"
- ❌ "Championed the development" → ✅ "Designed and delivered"
- ❌ "Championed the development of a custom engine" → ✅ "Built a shadow processing engine"

### The Real Gap: Part-Time Fit

The JD is for a **part-time** role. This CV screams "I was a full-time senior leader at a FAANG-tier company." 

**Missing:**
- Any consulting, contract, or fractional work
- Signal about WHY part-time is interesting
- Availability or bandwidth indicators

**Recommendation:** Add a one-line "Availability" note or cover letter explaining: *"Transitioning to fractional TPM consulting after 3 years leading teams at Mercado Libre. Available 20-30 hrs/week."*
